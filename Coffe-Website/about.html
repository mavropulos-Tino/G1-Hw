Types of Coffee

Espresso: concentrated coffee brewed by forcing hot water through finely-ground beans.
Latte: espresso with steamed milk.
Cappuccino: espresso, steamed milk, and a thick milk foam.
Macchiato: espresso “stained” with a small amount of milk or foam.

History of Coffee

Coffee’s story begins in the ancient highlands of Ethiopia, where legend tells of a goat herder who noticed his animals becoming unusually energetic after eating bright red berries. These berries eventually made their way across the Red Sea to Yemen, where monks brewed the first versions of the drink to help them stay awake during long nights of prayer.

Coffee Beans

Arabica

Generally sweeter and more aromatic, contains less caffeine.

Robusta

Stronger, more bitter, higher caffeine content and often used in espresso blends for crema.

Liberica

Known for their unique, bold, woody, smoky, and fruity flavor.

Brewing Methods

Espresso

  1. Grind beans fine.
  2. Dose 18-20 g (single ~9-10 g), tamp firmly.
  3. Brew with 9-10 bar pressure for ~25-30 s.
  4. Serve 25-40 ml.

Latte

  1. Pull one or two espresso shots.
  2. Steam milk until velvety microfoam forms.
  3. Pour milk over espresso, holding back foam, finish with thin foam layer.
  4. Serve in a tall cup.

Cappuccino

  1. Pull one or two espresso shots.
  2. Steam milk to a thicker, airy foam (more foam than a latte).
  3. Pour steamed milk and foam over espresso, aiming for ~1:1:1 espresso:milk:foam.
  4. Serve in a smaller cup.

Macchiato

  1. Pull a single espresso shot.
  2. Steam a small amount of milk to create thick foam.
  3. Spoon a dollop of foam onto the espresso (“stain” it).
  4. Serve immediately.

Health Benefits

Coffee is rich in antioxidants and can help boost energy, focus, and mood. Studies suggest it may support heart health, improve physical performance, and reduce the risk of certain diseases when consumed in moderation. It’s a simple daily drink that can offer both comfort and wellness benefits.

Coffee Around the World

Coffee culture varies widely across the globe. In Italy, espresso is enjoyed quickly at the bar; in Turkey, coffee is brewed thick and strong, often with sugar. Ethiopia celebrates coffee with traditional ceremonies, while Japan has mastered both pour-over precision and canned coffee convenience. Each country adds its own flavor, making coffee a truly global tradition.
